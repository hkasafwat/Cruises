A brief glance into the Worlds Cruise Lines:

Carnival Cruises

Carnival Cruises is owned by Carnival Corporation; the largest cruise company in the world who also own the likes of P&O Cruises, Holland America Line and Seabourn. Founded in 1972, Carnival Cruises have since introduced more than 20 new ships to their fleet. Carnival’s slogan is “Fun for all. All for fun”, and it is clear to see with all of their ships.

Celebrity Cruises

Celebrity Cruises offer luxury cruises that go beyond meeting your expectations, by offering a number of firsts for the cruising industry. Enjoy a family picnic on real grass, have fun and get fit at a zumba class or even relax in the Apple iLounge.

Cruise & Maritime Cruises

Specialising in no-fly cruising with convenient sailings from a choice of eight UK ports - Bristol Avonmouth, Edinburgh Leith & Rosyth, Glasgow Greenock, Hull, Liverpool, London Tilbury and Newcastle Upon Tyne. Cruise & Maritime Voyages offer a more traditional and leisurely 'country house' style of cruising and maritime experience aboard their classic cruise ships, Marco Polo, Azores, Astor and their new flagship Magellan.

Cunard

Cruising with Cunard is quite different to being on board any other Cruise Ship or even Cruise Line. All 3 of Cunard's Famous Ships incorporate a lot of grand traditions and maritime history. Queen Mary 2 was especially built to do Transatlantic crossings. By sailing this route and going on to explore the US East Coast and Canada, you will become part of Cunard’s history and will experience the same thrill evoked in passengers past.

Fred Olsen

Fred. Olsen Cruise Lines is all about tradition and as a family-run company with a long shipping heritage, the moment you step aboard either Balmoral, Braemar, Black Watch or Boudicca you are greeted with a personal and friendly service, which then continues throughout your cruise. Fred. Olsen’s ships are large enough to be spacious and uncrowded, but still small enough to be the kind of place where you can feel comfortable, relaxed and able to put your feet up.

Holland America Cruises

The luxury Holland America Line features an impressive fleet of 15 elegant vessels. World-renowned for offering a high quality, extravagant service, Holland America Line are rated as 5*. Holland America Line are easy to spot. They're characteristic design and notable ocean-style colour scheme just adds to their charm.

MSC Cruises

MSC Cruises have a long and impressive history of sailing. Having sailed the oceans for over 300 years, MSC class themselves as experts of life at sea. Enjoy mediterranean style cruising with beuatiful cusine, a high staff to passenger ratio and an eye for attention to detail. There are 12 elegant and technologically advanced ships with the MSC Preziosa being the latest of the fleet beginning service in June 2013.

NCL Cruises

With NCL as your cruise line of choice, you’re given the freedom to dress up or down with no schedules, set itineraries or meal times - Freestyle Cruising leaves every decision to you so you can choose how you want to cruise. It's no shock that they were voted "World's Best Top Mega Ship Cruise Lines" in 2013 and have catered to active holidaymakers since 1966.

P&O Cruises

About P&O Cruises P&O cruises have been a favourite for British passengers since they began way back in the 1850s. They are a quintessentially British line who have earned a legion of loyal fans through their attention to the needs to of their passengers and a focus on comfort. P&O cruises have 7 ships in their fleet: the newest being their brand new flagship Britannia which can hold over 4000 passengers.

Princess Cruises

Princess Cruises first set sail in 1965 with a single ship cruising to Mexico. 50 years later, Princess has grown to become the third largest cruise line in the world and a recognised leader in taking our guests to some of the most fascinating places in the world. We have a wealth of experience and knowledge about the destinations we visit and you can trust Princess to deliver the holiday you dream of every time.

Royal carribean

Royal Caribbean Cruises provide an extensive variety of cruise holidays to all holidaymakers. They currently have a fleet of 23 ships on the seas, which is however increasing, - the array of schedules, activities and facilities will appeal to all age groups, families and tastes. Offering so much more than a regular cruise company; Royal Caribbean is popular with both new and regular cruisers, and is often referred to as one of the industry's greatest.

Thomson Cruises

Thomson Cruises are perfect for the British market and unlike alot of cruise lines, they use pound stirling on board so there's no need to exchange your money before embarking. They also own their own airline called 'Thomsonfly' which makes getting to your cruise even easier, with a choice of 21 regional airports to fly from you're never to far away from home!
